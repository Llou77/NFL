Confidence Calibration

Are higher-confidence predictions actually more accurate?

What confidence measures: Not win probability. It measures how much the model trusts its own prediction given data quality and internal sub-model agreement. A well-calibrated confidence system means HIGH confidence predictions should have lower average errors than LOW confidence ones.

Betting Edge Analysis

When the model and Vegas disagree — who is right?

Important context: The model's overall "spread direction accuracy" (~90%) is misleading — it simply reflects that our predicted margins correlate with actual outcomes. The real signal is what happens on games where the model and Vegas favor different teams entirely. That is shown below.
True betting edge hit rate — games where model and Vegas favored different teams
Break-even point: With standard -110 odds (the most common NFL betting line), you need to be right 52.4% of the time just to break even. Any sustained rate above 55% is genuinely meaningful and rare even among professional handicappers.
